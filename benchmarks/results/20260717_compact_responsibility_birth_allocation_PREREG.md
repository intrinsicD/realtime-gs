## Literal point compositor and scalable responsibility seam

camera-space center depth, the established point renderer computes:

```text

g_ki     = kernel_support_weight(q_ki, mode="hard")
alpha_ki = clamp(opacity_i * g_ki, 0, 0.999)
T_k0     = 1
T_ki     = product_{j<i} (1 - alpha_kj + 1e-10)
w_ki     = alpha_ki * T_ki
```

## Exact score arithmetic

Let $K=128$, $V=7$, and let each view occur exactly $C=5$ times in steps 1--35.

The optimized step loss is exactly:

$$
L_t=\frac{1}{K}\sum_{k=1}^{K} a_{tk}
\left[\frac{1}{3}\sum_{c=1}^{3}
\left(P_{tkc}-T_{tkc}\right)^2\right].
$$

This is the existing `fixed_attempt_mean` under `proposal_attempt`: active importance is one and
inactive importance is zero. It is intentionally an unnormalized proposal-attempt submeasure.

For current parent $i$, the VJP supplies:

$$
r_{ti}=\frac{1}{K}\sum_k a_{tk}e_{tk}w_{tki},\qquad
s_{ti}=\frac{1}{K}\sum_k a_{tk}w_{tki}.
$$

The native VJP reduction is float32. The reduced numerator is then cast to float64 before
division by 128 and every later accumulation. For view $v$:

$$
R_{vi}=\frac{1}{C}\sum_{t:\,v_t=v}r_{ti},\qquad
S_{vi}=\frac{1}{C}\sum_{t:\,v_t=v}s_{ti}.
$$

The authoritative equal-view scores are:

$$
R_i=\frac{1}{V}\sum_{v=1}^{V}R_{vi},\qquad
S_i=\frac{1}{V}\sum_{v=1}^{V}S_{vi}.
$$

No view is dropped or reweighted. A parent absent from one view contributes exact zero for that
view. Serialize every per-step native VJP reduction, per-view float64 subtotal, equal-view score,
positive-support view count, visible-step count, and hash.

The ordinary comparator is the current native-float32 screen-gradient score. After the one normal
backward at step $t$:

$$
g_{ti}=
\left\|\frac{\partial L_t}{\partial \mathrm{mean2d}_{ti}}\right\|_2
\frac{\max(W_v,H_v)}{2}.
$$

The multiplication in the displayed formula is intended; executable arithmetic is
`norm * (max(width,height) * 0.5)`. Accumulate native float32 with the current indexed-add
semantics, increment the denominator once for every coarse-visible step, and compute:

$$
G_i=\frac{\sum_{t:\,i\ \mathrm{coarse-visible}}g_{ti}}
{\max(\#\{t:i\ \mathrm{coarse-visible}\},1)}.
$$

Coarse visibility alone does not make a row eligible. A separate float64 reconstruction and
equal-view gradient diagnostic may be serialized but cannot rank, select, or break a tie.

The pooled assigned-residual fraction is:

$$
F_{\mathrm{assigned}}=
\frac{\sum_{t,k}a_{tk}e_{tk}\sum_i w_{tki}}
{\sum_{t,k}a_{tk}e_{tk}}.
$$

Use the same native VJP numerators cast to float64 for the numerator and detached native
$a_ke_k$ values cast to float64 for the denominator. The denominator must be finite and positive.

## Phase B — paired matched-count utility

Every Phase-B arm starts from the common initialization and uses one persistent 140-update trainer
call. It replays steps 1--35 rather than loading a mutable Phase-A optimizer object. Immediately
before surgery it must exactly match Phase A on:

- all six parameter tensors and ordering;

- losses and gradients;

Any mismatch fails before surgery. Phase B cannot “repair” a replay mismatch by loading the Phase-A
state.

All three arms within a replicate must match every one of the 140 view indices, sample seeds,
coordinates, active/inside flags, component IDs, proposal densities, joint densities, target
densities, and importances. Because topology differs after step 35, prediction/loss/gradient
hashes are expected to differ. Split standard-normal arrays remain identical.

Fresh fixed evaluation banks are fully materialized and hash-bound before the first Phase-B arm:
for each evaluation root and each of seven views, exactly 4,096 uniform attempts and 4,096
proposal attempts. Uniform banks are direct, fully active, finite, inside the fitted window, and
strictly half-open. Proposal banks retain every null attempt. A bank may never be generated,
discarded, or replaced after seeing an arm.

The fixed-bank risks are:

$$
J_U=\frac1{4096}\sum_k\ell_k,\qquad
J_Q=\frac1{4096}\sum_k\mathbf1[\mathrm{active}_k]\ell_k,
$$

where $\ell_k$ is RGB-channel mean squared error between the point-rendered student and exact
compact teacher. Accumulate loss in float64 from detached native-float32 predictions/targets and
average views equally. Never divide $J_Q$ by active count.

Evaluate:

```text
0, 35_pre, 35_post, 70, 105, 140
```

Step 0 and `35_pre` are common. In the first arm of a replicate, freeze all selections and state
hashes before passing a detached snapshot to the evaluator. Bind and reuse those two common
metrics for later arms only after their state hashes match. The evaluator returns no value to the
controller or optimizer. `35_post` is arm-specific and is not replaced by `35_pre`.

The authorizing recovery curve contains only:

```text
steps = [35_post,70,105,140]
x     = [0,1/3,2/3,1]
```

For positive finite risks:

$$
\operatorname{logAUC}(J)=
\sum_{j=0}^{2}(x_{j+1}-x_j)
\frac{\log(\max(J_j,10^{-12}))+\log(\max(J_{j+1},10^{-12}))}{2}.
$$

For numerator arm R and comparator C, the AUC-derived ratio is
`exp(logAUC(R)-logAUC(C))`.

## Sole utility decision and safety gates

Compute all ratios from unrounded values in training-root order
`77001,77002,77003`. For each comparator `C in {G,U}`, R must pass **all**:

1. geometric-mean final-$J_Q$ ratio `R/C <= 0.97`;

3. strict final-$J_Q$ wins in at least two of three paired roots;
4. geometric-mean final-$J_U$ ratio `R/C <= 1.05`; and
5. every-root final-$J_U$ ratio `R/C <= 1.10`.

For a vector of finite positive ratios $x_s$, geometric mean is exactly
`exp(fmean(log(x_s)))`. Strict wins use unrounded `J_Q_R < J_Q_C`.

- every one of the 21 proposal-bank active fractions is at least `0.95`; and
- global maximum/minimum active fraction is at most `1.03`.
