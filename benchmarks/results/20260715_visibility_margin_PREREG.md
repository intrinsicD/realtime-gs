# Coarse visibility-margin incidence and support-safe bound preregistration

## Chronology, motivation, and scope

Frozen at `2026-07-15T22:46:25+02:00`, before implementing the margin option or computing any
coarse-cull incidence. The independently audited C1 kernel-tail experiment established that local
annulus gradient passed its mechanism gate but that both the fixed forward taper and exact
hard-forward gradient control produced 0/3 diffuse PSNR wins. That branch is closed without tuning.

Pre-seal amendment at `2026-07-15T22:56+02:00`, still before any official incidence, training, or
candidate outcome: independent protocol review identified two coupled validity requirements.
First, because this intervention corrects forward visibility as well as the training graph,
matched-by-arm final rendering must measure its primary total effect; rendering both arms with the
old cull would erase exactly the edge-support contribution under test. Second, the synthetic
targets were generated by the current renderer, so current-versus-support-safe rendering of every
frozen ground-truth scene must be exactly identical before any fit, incidence audit, or training.
The prerequisite and Phase-B metrics below were amended accordingly; scenes, seeds, thresholds,
and the intervention itself were not changed.

The next operation is a distinct set-level discontinuity. The Torch reference renderer culls a
projected Gaussian when its center lies outside the image expanded by
`3*sqrt(lambda_max(Sigma_2D))`, but its established per-pixel hard support is `q < 12`. Since
`q >= ||d||^2/lambda_max`, a conservative support-safe image margin is
`sqrt(12)*sqrt(lambda_max)`, not `3*sqrt(lambda_max)`. The current cull can therefore omit genuine
kernel support only in the narrow geometric shell corresponding to `9 <= q < 12`.

The 2026-07-12 through 2026-07-15 Scholar Inbox digest contains no paper directly evaluating this
3DGS raster-culling mismatch. Grassmannian Splatting I (arXiv:2607.10489) smooths an unrelated
Schur denominator, while SplatCtrl (arXiv:2607.08948) uses continuous Gaussian distance fields for
control/collision queries. Neither is evidence for a raster-margin change. The proposed candidate
is an exact conservative bound derived from the existing kernel, not a learned or swept smooth
activation.

This is a final-state, fixed-topology CPU synthetic audit of the Torch reference backend. It cannot
establish real-scene utility, gsplat/CUDA tile-culling behavior or speed, density interaction,
near-plane behavior, or a default change.

## Frozen intervention and data

1. `current`: unchanged detached visibility margin multiplier `3.0`.
2. `support_safe`: detached multiplier `sqrt(12)` with the same near plane, projected covariance,
   eigenvalue guard, hard `q<12` kernel, alpha cap, sorting, and losses.

The default remains exactly `3.0`. The option is Torch-only; gsplat must explicitly reject a
non-default margin. No margin sweep is permitted.

Reuse seeds `0,1,2`, the `diffuse` and `view_dependent` scenes, 40 GT splats, twelve 48x48 cameras,
train split `[0,1,2,4,5,6,8,9,10]`, held-out `[3,7,11]`, native fit, metric-depth surface lift,
and 120-step density-disabled hard refinement exactly as incorporated by
`benchmarks/results/20260715_kernel_support_taper_PREREG.md` at SHA-256
`c78a74ea67a4a0d327b8ef884006dc8ad5781da9a632f557c2e9f370a8868a58`. Every arm/diagnostic
reuses paired scene, fitted-set, initialization, and view-schedule hashes.

Before fitting either condition, render its ground-truth Gaussians in all twelve cameras with both
margin modes. Require exact tensor equality for current versus support-safe color, alpha, and
accumulated depth; also require the current color after the established clamp and expected-depth
construction to equal the stored synthetic targets. Record hashes and maximum absolute errors.
Any mismatch invalidates the experiment before Phase A: it would mean the frozen targets encode
the truncation being tested and would therefore bias a matched support-safe candidate. This is a
validity prerequisite, not an incidence or utility gate.

## Phase A: final-state hard-run incidence

Exactly one current-margin hard refinement is run for each condition/seed. The ordinary held-out
hard metrics are recorded, but only training views contribute to the gate. At both the pinned
initialization and final parameters, and for every training view, compute projected covariance and
the two detached coarse-visible sets without using target RGB. Assert current-visible is a subset
of support-safe-visible, every newly admitted Gaussian is in front of the unchanged near plane,
and the current-visible depth order exactly equals the support-safe order filtered to current
indices.

For all pixels and **all in-front Gaussians**, compute squared Mahalanobis distance and define
`U = {in-front and q<12}` independently of either coarse cull. Assert every Gaussian participating
in `U` is support-safe-visible. Then define `I = {current-visible and q<12}` and
`M = {support-safe-visible, not current-visible, q<12}` and assert `I` and `M` are a disjoint,
complete partition of `U`. Assert every `M` pair has `q >= 9-1e-5`. Aggregate raw float64:

- pair counts and `missed_pair_fraction = |M| / |I union M|`;
- distinct missed Gaussian/view exposures;
- effective mass `opacity*k(q)` and
  `missed_effective_mass_fraction = sum_M(opacity*k) / sum_(I union M)(opacity*k)`;
- per-view current and support-safe color/alpha/depth renders, requiring finite outputs;
- `render_delta_over_residual = sum|safe.color-current.color| /
  sum|current.color-target|` on raw, unclamped renderer and training RGB;
- current and support-safe frozen training objective
  `0.8*mean_abs_rgb + 0.2*(1-SSIM)` on raw renderer RGB, and
  `signed_relative_objective_change = (sum L_safe - sum L_current) / sum L_current`;
- missed counts/mass by q bins `[9,10),[10,11),[11,12)` and by view.

A pooled distinct exposure is one unique `(seed, view, gaussian_index)` tuple; it is not a pixel
pair and is never summed from already-aggregated seed percentages.

The normal hard training graph and optimizer use only `current`; all Phase-A counterfactuals run
under no-grad after initialization/final checkpoints. Projection, covariance, inverse, `q`, kernel,
render, and aggregate tensors must all be finite. Ratios pool raw numerators/denominators, not seed
percentages. Any non-finite/zero denominator or subset/shell invariant failure invalidates the
audit.

Phase B is authorized only when every final diffuse seed first passes both validity prerequisites:
all nine training views were audited and at least 100,000 `U` support pairs were audited. The raw
pool must consequently contain at least 300,000 support pairs. Subject to those prerequisites, the
final diffuse aggregate must pass all four material gates independently in at least two of three
seeds and independently in the raw-sum pool:

1. `missed_pair_fraction >= 0.0005`;
2. `missed_effective_mass_fraction >= 0.0005`;
3. `render_delta_over_residual >= 0.001`;
4. at least 100 missed support pairs and at least three distinct missed Gaussian/view exposures.

The signed objective change is diagnostic, not a gate: an untrained exact-support correction may
initially help or hurt. Initialization and view-dependent statistics are reporting-only and cannot
rescue a failed final diffuse gate. If the gate fails, stop without changing the margin, gates,
scene framing, resolution, or seeds.

## Phase B: paired support-safe training

Only after a passing Phase A and independent strict review, train `support_safe` from each paired
initialization under the otherwise identical refinement config and schedule. The Phase-A current
run remains the baseline. Verify step-zero current renders are exactly the established default,
initial fields/configs/schedules/checkpoints/counts match, and every parameter/render/history is
finite.

Final evaluation records the full 2x2 matrix: current-trained/current-rendered baseline,
current-trained/support-safe-rendered forward-only attribution, support-safe-trained/current-
rendered training-only attribution, and support-safe-trained/support-safe-rendered matched total
effect. Primary evaluation is the matched-by-arm total effect. One fixed ground-truth support/depth
reference from the parity-validated current renderer is used for every comparison. On diffuse,
support-safe must improve mean matched foreground PSNR by at least `0.10 dB`
with at least 2/3 seed wins; matched mean SSIM may regress at most `0.002` and no seed more than
`0.005`; matched normalized expected-depth RMSE may regress at most `2%`; matched alpha IoU and
coverage may regress at most `0.02`. The candidate is also rendered with the common current margin
as a training-effect attribution control, and its diffuse foreground PSNR may regress by at most
`0.10 dB` in the mean and `0.25 dB` in any seed. View-dependent matched mean PSNR may regress at
most `0.10 dB` and no seed more than `0.25 dB`; its common-current counterpart is reporting-only.
Guardrails and attribution cannot rescue failed matched PSNR utility.

## Interpretation and artifacts

- Phase-A failure: current 3-sigma culling is not materially truncating support in this setup;
  retain it and do not tune margins.
- Phase-A pass but Phase-B fail: the geometric mismatch exists materially, but the exact
  support-safe bound has no demonstrated training utility here.
- Phase-B pass: only a promising matched CPU reference-renderer correction; require separate
  real-scene, gsplat/CUDA semantics/performance, and density-enabled confirmation before a default
  change.
